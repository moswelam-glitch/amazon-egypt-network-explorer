🏪 HCD3 — Amazon Now (Dark Stores)

Ultra-fast delivery ecosystem achieving 13–17 minutes from order to doorstep. Operates through dark stores (400–500 sqm, 8 associates/shift) fed by the Regional Distribution Center, using a pick-to-ship model with no separate packing step.

📦 SPX9 — Storage Distribution Center (SDC)

External storage backbone for Fulfillment Centers handling items with vendor lead times exceeding 21 days. Feeds replenishment inventory to HCD3 dark stores and CAI6 on a scheduled, forecast-driven cadence.

FC — Inbound Operations

The 3-step inbound sequence: Dock → Receive → Stow. Each item progresses through defined system states before becoming available for picking.

1 DOCK

The dock is the entry point for all vendor and supplier shipments. The associate opens Dock Master to monitor shipment status through defined stages: Scheduled → Arrived → Closed.

Gate Check-In: Truck arrives at facility gate, shipment verified for timeliness. Late arrivals flagged and held pending dock availability.

Yard & Dock Assignment: Trailer assigned a yard spot, directed to dock entrance. TDR (Trailer Door Release) must be set before any unloading begins.

Unloading: Dock ambassador counts and scans every item (ASIN/barcode). System accepts shortages but does NOT receive excess quantities. Shipments arrive as pallets or cartons.

Confirmation: Vendor receives signed/stamped document. Shipment marked as closed in Dock Master and moved to NYR (Not Yet Received) storage.

2 RECEIVE

Items are formally inducted into the FC's inventory system. Associate determines receive type: Each | Prep | Case | Pallet.

Product Scanning: Associate scans product code (ASIN/barcode) to verify physical item matches system specs. System validates dimensions, weight, and specifications in real time.

Expiry Check: For applicable categories (groceries, cosmetics), expiry dates verified. Items without visible expiry are escalated to Problem Solve team for assessment.

Trolley Placement: Successfully scanned items placed on designated trolley. Status changes to NYS (Not Yet Stowed) — received and awaiting placement.

3 STOW

The final inbound stage — items physically placed into designated storage locations.

Setup: Associate scans drop zone → trolley to confirm assigned items.

Location Selection: System recommends storage location based on item height and weight. Associate scans location barcode to confirm before placement.

Rules Enforced:

• BOAATS Rule — bin organization, orientation, and placement compliance

• ASIN Limits Per Shelf — maximum ASINs per bin to prevent overcrowding

Reactive Stowing: Special priority mode for transshipments from SPX5 — items destined for active orders are stowed first for immediate picking.

Completion: Item status updates to Available — live and pickable for outbound orders.

FC — Outbound Operations

The 5-step outbound sequence: Pick → Wrangle → Sort → Pack → Dock. Orders move from storage to customer shipment.

1 PICK

Associate logs in and is assigned a picking path/batch. System groups orders to minimize walking distance.

Process: System directs to exact storage location → associate scans location barcode → picks required quantity → scans item (ASIN/barcode) to validate → places into assigned tote/cart.

Handoff: Completed totes sent via conveyor to Wrangle.

2 WRANGLE

Receiving and organization stage for picked totes arriving from the pick floor.

Process: Totes arrive via conveyor → each tote scanned to confirm identity → placed into assigned space grouped by batch. Acts as a buffer and consolidation checkpoint between Pick and Sort.

3 SORT

Items from multi-item orders organized by customer order using a rebin grid.

Process: Associate prepares rebin (size based on batch) → scans each tote → system assigns items to specific rebin slots by order → items physically placed into slots until all are sorted.

Result: Each rebin slot contains all items for one customer order — consolidated and ready for packing.

4 PACK

Consolidated orders placed into final packaging for shipment.

Process: Associate scans zone and rebin → scans each item → system suggests optimal packaging type (box sizes, poly bags, padded envelopes) → items packed and sealed.

SLAM Stage: Package passes through Scan, Label, Apply, Manifest — system generates shipping label, applies it, and adds to outbound manifest.

5 DOCK (Outbound)

Final stage — packed shipments loaded onto trucks for dispatch.

Pallet Building: Associates stack packages efficiently, maximizing density while maintaining stability. Experience critical for spatial optimization.

Securing: Pallets wrapped with shrink wrap (required compliance step before loading).

Dispatch: Correct pallets loaded onto correct trucks aligned with routing destinations. Manifest closed, truck sealed, departure recorded.

FC — ReLo (Returns & Logistics)

CRET manages customer returns. VRET manages vendor returns. Together they handle all reverse inventory flows at the FC level.

Handles all aspects of returned inventory — from problem resolution and quality auditing to item restoration and technical assessment.

VRET — Vendor Returns

Manages the return of unhealthy, unsellable, or excess inventory back to vendors or through liquidation/destruction channels.

How VRET Works

The FC system identifies unhealthy inventory on a daily basis — items that are damaged, expired, recalled, or flagged by quality processes. These items are pulled from sellable inventory and routed through the V-Ret process.

Process Flow:

Identify Unhealthy Inventory → Pick → Sort / Rebin → Pack → Route to Destination

Destinations:

• Vendor Return — items sent back to original vendor per agreement

• Liquidation — sold through secondary channels at reduced price

• Donate — eligible items donated to partner organizations

• Destroy — items that cannot be resold, donated, or returned

Key Notes: Hot picks are NOT generated for FRACS items. LTL (Less Than Truckload) shipments skip the rebin stage and go directly to pack.

Forward Process — Shipment Flow
First Mile (FM) → Sorting Center → Delivery Station → Customer

Shipments are scanned at inbound dock, sorted by seller location, and dispatched to the Sorting Center. CPT of 14:00 must be met for same-day delivery eligibility.

Reverse Process — Customer Returns
Customer → Delivery Station → Sorting Center → First Mile (SPX5)

Returns are routed through the Sorting Center (rather than direct to FM) to reduce shipping costs. All customer returns are processed at SPX5 via CRET operations.

Return to Seller Process

Shipments prepared on palettes and manually scanned into a Quip tracker as a second verification gate for double-checking accuracy.

⚠️ Pain Point: Manual scanning is time-consuming and error-prone — identified as a HIGH priority automation opportunity.

Middle Mile — Sorting Center Operations

Central sorting and reverse logistics hub for the network. Processes all inbound channels and routes to outbound destinations within a 2-hour SLA.

Facility Profile

Example: ~7,000 cubic meters capacity
  • 4 dock doors serving 9 working docks
  • SLA: 2 hours per shipment (receipt to outbound lane)
  • Dual-lane conveyor system (Flyers + Boxes)
  • VSM code routing with 3-5% wrong-sort tolerance

Inbound Channels

3 distinct inbound sources
  • FC: Pre-applied labels, uniform packaging — fastest channel
  • Vendor Flex: Extra label verification needed (+15-30s/pkg)
  • First Mile: Full intake processing (weight + dimension) — most time-intensive

Outbound Destinations

3 destination types with distinct protocols
  • Delivery Stations: Dispatched at volume threshold or CPT
  • EDSP: Separate manifest reconciliation before handoff
  • AMZL: Priority-sorted, tighter SLA, loaded LIFO
Sorting Process — Conveyor System

Dual-Lane Design: Right lane handles Flyers (soft/poly-bag packages, gentle handling). Left lane handles Boxes (rigid cartons, physical pushers at exit points).

Sweeper Run Protocol

Trigger: Before CPT when pallet accumulation >8 pallets OR floor lead decision.

Duration: ~15-20 minutes. Systematic clearing from injection end toward outbound staging.

Purpose: Non-negotiable pre-CPT checkpoint — guarantees sort floor cleared before outbound loading begins.
